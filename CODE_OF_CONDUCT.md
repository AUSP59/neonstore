## NeonStore — Community Code of Conduct (v2.1+)

> Short version: Be respectful. Be kind. Be responsible. No harassment. Use inclusive language. Help each other succeed.

### Our Pledge
We, as maintainers and contributors, pledge to make participation in NeonStore a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

### Our Standards
Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:
- Using welcoming and inclusive language; being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the project.
- Showing empathy toward other community members.
- Following project processes (issue templates, PR reviews, security reporting).

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:
- Harassment, discrimination, or derogatory comments (public or private).
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory remarks, personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment, doxxing, or publishing others’ private information without explicit permission.
- Sexualized language or imagery; unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, PRs, reviews, or meetings.
- Any conduct that violates applicable law, export controls, or third-party IP/licensing.

### Responsibilities & Scope
- **Maintainers** are responsible for clarifying standards, taking corrective action, and communicating reasons for moderation decisions.
- This Code applies **within all project spaces** (GitHub issues, PRs, discussions, code review, wiki/docs, CI logs, chats, meetings) and **when an individual is officially representing the project** in public spaces.

### Reporting a Concern
If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it as soon as possible to the Enforcement Contact:

- **Primary:** Alan U. — <alanursapu@gmail.com> (GitHub: **@AUSP59**)

Reports should include: your contact info (optional), names of people involved (if known), description of what happened, date/time and context (links/screenshots if possible), and whether it is ongoing. We will acknowledge receipt within **3 business days** and aim to resolve or provide an update within **10 business days**.

#### Confidentiality & Safety
We will keep reporters’ identities confidential to the extent possible and will not retaliate against good-faith reports. False or bad-faith reports may themselves violate this Code.

#### Conflict of Interest
If the report involves a maintainer, that person will **recuse** from handling the incident. If you prefer, you may request an external reviewer or escalation to another maintainer.

### Enforcement Guidelines
Maintainers will follow these Community Impact Guidelines to determine consequences:

1. **Correction**  
   - *Impact:* Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional but minor.  
   - *Consequence:* Private, written warning; education on standards; request to change or remove content.

2. **Warning**  
   - *Impact:* A single incident or pattern of behavior that violates the Code.  
   - *Consequence:* Formal warning; continued violations may lead to temporary restrictions. Required acknowledgment of this CoC.

3. **Temporary Restriction**  
   - *Impact:* Major or repeated violations (e.g., harassment).  
   - *Consequence:* Temporary ban from interactions and contributions for a defined period; conditions for reinstatement provided.

4. **Permanent Ban**  
   - *Impact:* Demonstrated pattern of harassment, abuse, or threats; refusal to comply after warnings.  
   - *Consequence:* Permanent removal from project spaces and, if applicable, revocation of organization membership.

**Appeals:** You may appeal an enforcement decision by replying to the decision email with additional context. A different maintainer will review the appeal.

### Moderation Tools
Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments; lock threads; request changes; close or refuse PRs/issues; or escalate to platform administrators (e.g., GitHub Support) where necessary.
